863 El Camino Real
Side of bldg
24"x18"Menlo GatesNone
Irish immigrants Dennis J. Oliver and his brother in law D.C. McGlynn were Menlo Park's first permanent settlers in 1853. They found the tree-studded pastureland of this area reminiscent of "Menlough," their hometown located on the outskirts of Galway, Ireland. This led them to purchase a 1,700-acre tract of land on the southern end of the Pulgas Grant, land on which you are now standing.
In 1854 Oliver and McGlynn erected a magnificent, wooden gateway to their property. The name "Menlo Park" was written on the center, main arch in foot-high letters. The gates, as pictured above, stood on the west side of El Camino Real adjacent to this marker. Unfortunately, a motorist demolished the gates in 1922.
This marker is presented to the City of Menlo Park on the occasion of the Sesquicentennial of the County of San Mateo in recognition of the county's incorporation on April 19, 1856, by the Rotary Club of Menlo Park, Kiwanis Club, Lions Club, Menlo Park Chamber of Commerce and the Historical Association of Menlo Park.

Note: In 2019 this plaque temporarily removed pending decision on where to relocate.

Catherine Birdsall Johnson
1834-1892


Over 100 years ago Kate Johnson's home, "Heart's Ease," stood on this site.
Her long front porch, lined with a dozen rockers and chairs, was no doubt
a restful haven for Kate and her 50 or so cats.


In addition to her great affection for cats, Kate has a special sensitivity for mothers,
children and the poor. Her generous and charitable nature led to the building of
Mary's Help Hospital in San Francisco as well as the deeding of property
for St. Patrick's Seminary (directly behind you).
